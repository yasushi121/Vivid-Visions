"I dream my painting, and then I paint my dream." – Vincent van Gogh

Discussing the importance of an artwork, particularly renowned masterpieces, is essential for understanding the evolution of human culture, thought, and emotion. Paintings serve as visual narratives that encapsulate the values, beliefs, and experiences of the societies from which they emerged. For instance, Vincent van Gogh's The Starry Night (1889) stands as a profound testament to the power of art to convey the depths of human emotion and the mysteries of the natural world. Beyond its emotional depth, The Starry Night has intrigued scientists and art historians alike. Recent analyses have revealed that the patterns in the sky align with mathematical models of turbulence.
